## Legal Framework

### ADA Title III Requirements
- **Places of Public Accommodation** must be accessible
- **Websites integrated with physical locations** are covered
- **No specific technical standards** mandated by ADA
- **Courts reference WCAG 2.1 AA** as the de facto standard

### Key Court Rulings
- **Winn-Dixie (2017):** Website-to-store integration creates ADA obligations
- **Domino's (2019):** Supreme Court declined review, affirming accessibility requirements
- **11th Circuit (2018):** Websites connected to physical locations subject to ADA

## Common Violation Patterns

### Top 10 Issues ADA Trolls Target

#### 1. **Missing Alt Text for Images**
- **What they look for:** Images without descriptive alt attributes
- **Impact:** Screen readers can't describe images to blind users
- **Risk Level:** 🔴 HIGH - Found in 67% of lawsuits

#### 2. **Inadequate Form Labels**
- **What they look for:** Form fields without proper labels or instructions
- **Impact:** Users can't understand what information to enter
- **Risk Level:** 🔴 HIGH - Found in 59% of lawsuits

#### 3. **Poor Color Contrast**
- **What they look for:** Text that doesn't meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- **Impact:** Users with visual impairments can't read content
- **Risk Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM - Found in 83% of sites but harder to prove damages

#### 4. **Keyboard Navigation Failures**
- **What they look for:** Interactive elements not accessible via keyboard
- **Impact:** Users who can't use a mouse are locked out
- **Risk Level:** 🔴 HIGH - Found in 55% of lawsuits

#### 5. **Missing Skip Links**
- **What they look for:** No way to skip repetitive navigation
- **Impact:** Keyboard users must tab through entire nav each page
- **Risk Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM - Easy to implement fix

#### 6. **Inaccessible Dropdown Menus**
- **What they look for:** Dropdowns that don't work with screen readers
- **Impact:** Users can't access navigation or form options
- **Risk Level:** 🔴 HIGH - Navigation is critical functionality

#### 7. **Auto-Playing Media**
- **What they look for:** Videos/audio that start automatically
- **Impact:** Interferes with screen readers, causes disorientation
- **Risk Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM - But easy to fix

#### 8. **Missing Focus Indicators**
- **What they look for:** No visual indication of keyboard focus
- **Impact:** Keyboard users can't see where they are on page
- **Risk Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM - Affects user experience significantly

#### 9. **Inaccessible PDF Documents**
- **What they look for:** PDFs without proper structure/tags
- **Impact:** Screen readers can't navigate document content
- **Risk Level:** 🔴 HIGH - Legal documents must be accessible

#### 10. **Missing Page Titles and Headings**
- **What they look for:** Pages without descriptive titles or proper heading structure
- **Impact:** Users can't understand page content or navigate efficiently
- **Risk Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM - Affects SEO too

## WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance Checklist

### Level A (Minimum)
- [ ] **Images have alt text**
- [ ] **Videos have captions**
- [ ] **Content is keyboard accessible**
- [ ] **No seizure-inducing content**
- [ ] **Page has proper title**

### Level AA (Standard)
- [ ] **Color contrast ratio 4.5:1** (3:1 for large text)
- [ ] **Text can resize to 200%** without horizontal scrolling
- [ ] **Focus is visible** on all interactive elements
- [ ] **Headings are properly structured** (H1, H2, H3 hierarchy)
- [ ] **Form labels are associated** with their inputs

### Level AAA (Gold Standard)
- [ ] **Color contrast ratio 7:1** (4.5:1 for large text)
- [ ] **No time limits** on content
- [ ] **Context-sensitive help** available
- [ ] **Error prevention** for legal/financial transactions
